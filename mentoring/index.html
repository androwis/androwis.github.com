//androwis/mentoring

Part of a distributed mentoring movement.

Every major step forward in my life so far has been inspired and encouraged by the mentors I’ve been fortunate enough to have. I believe that hopes, dreams, and advice are best shared in ongoing, personal relationships, and it’s important to me to make myself available as a mentor to others.

I’ve had so many great experiences in my life, and I’d love to share them if you’re considering pursuing a similar path.

  • 2003-2006, I explored my love for art and design through the help of Wayland Shamburger of Shamburger Design Studio

  • 2005-2009 were some of the best years of my life, as I explored the wild world of stocks with Dr. Boone Turchi and numerical analysis with Dr. Sorin Mitran [this man is a genius] while falling in love with computer languages thanks to Dr. Jeff Terrell

  • 2008 marked my introduction into assistive technology as Dr. Gary Bishop and I explored the use of an online auditory interface as a new assistive technology for the blind

  • I started Scenedipity in 2011 as an open data platform for restaurant and food truck information. This has been my introduction to and playground for the NoSQL movement with the help of Justin Shoffstall who currently works for Basho

  • I'm currently Director of Engineering at Simply Home where I develop assistive technologies and business solutions for people with disabilities. I'm also a Ph.D. candidate at the University of Rochester under the advising of Dr. Jeff Bigham as I continue my study of dynamic user interfaces and artificial intelligence

If you’d like to work together, you can write me a letter responding to the questions below; I’ll write back to all letters I receive in this way within two weeks no matter what. In response to the most exceptional letters, I’ll propose further correspondence—and in certain cases, an ongoing mentoring relationship.

1. Tell me about yourself. How did you become who you are, and who do you want to become?

2. What do you hope to gain from our mentoring relationship?

3. What’s the best thing you’ve read, watched, or listened to lately?

Add links to any profiles or projects you’re proud of, and send it all to:
androwis@androw.is

I look forward to learning together!
